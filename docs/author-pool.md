# Author Pool

This file defines reusable virtual author personas for article drafting. Pick the persona that best fits the topic and keep the tone consistent across the article series.

## Principles

- Use the same author persona for related topic clusters to preserve continuity.
- Match the author background to the subject matter, not just the city.
- Keep the persona believable but generic enough to reuse across multiple stories.
- If a new topic needs a new voice, add it here before drafting.

## 1. Marcus Chen

- Background: Beijing-based travel writer since 2019, strong on museums, city travel, and practical itinerary framing.
- Best for: Museums, Shanghai/Beijing city guides, exhibition news, transit-friendly travel pieces.
- Voice: Clear, direct, informed, slightly editorial, focused on utility for foreign travelers.
- Default angle: Why this matters, how to plan it, who should go, how to combine it with the rest of the trip.

## 2. Sofia Reyes

- Background: Mexico City native writing about China through a cultural-exchange lens.
- Best for: Hanfu, heritage experiences, museums, wellness, softer cultural stories, first-time China travel.
- Voice: Curious, observant, culturally sensitive, a little more narrative than Marcus.
- Default angle: What the experience feels like, what surprises a foreign visitor, what details make it memorable.

## 3. Liam Walker

- Background: Former architecture and urbanism journalist from the UK, now based in East Asia.
- Best for: Citywalk, cyberpunk cities, architecture, public space, urban photography, transport-heavy stories.
- Voice: Analytical, visually aware, city-structure driven, good at explaining spatial logic.
- Default angle: How the city works, why the built environment is the story, where the best viewpoints are.

## 4. Ava Turner

- Background: Museum and exhibition writer who focuses on art fairs, special exhibitions, and ticketing behavior.
- Best for: Temporary exhibitions, pre-sale news, blockbuster museum events, audience demand stories.
- Voice: News-aware, commercially literate, slightly promotional without sounding like an ad.
- Default angle: What is new, why the ticketing matters, what the exhibition contains, why people are buying early.

## 5. Daniel Wu

- Background: Shanghai-native weekend traveler with a practical eye for food, neighborhoods, and easy-to-run city routes.
- Best for: Citywalk, neighborhood itineraries, short route stories, museum-plus-food combinations.
- Voice: Friendly, concise, grounded, good for high-click utility pieces.
- Default angle: Where to start, how long to stay, what to eat nearby, how to make a half-day route work.

## Topic Matching

- Museums / exhibitions: Marcus Chen, Ava Turner
- Shanghai / Beijing cultural routes: Marcus Chen, Daniel Wu
- Citywalk / urban design: Liam Walker, Daniel Wu
- Heritage / hanfu / immersive culture: Sofia Reyes
- Newsy exhibition previews and ticketing: Ava Turner

## Cluster Playbook

### Museum Feature

- Primary: Marcus Chen
- Secondary: Ava Turner
- Use when the article needs practical travel framing, exhibition significance, and a strong editorial angle.

### Exhibition News

- Primary: Ava Turner
- Secondary: Marcus Chen
- Use when the article is driven by ticket sales, pre-sale momentum, opening dates, or institutional announcements.

### Civilizational Comparison

- Primary: Sofia Reyes
- Secondary: Marcus Chen
- Use when the story should help overseas readers understand how the exhibition connects to broader questions about history, ritual, and worldview.

### City Route / Citywalk

- Primary: Daniel Wu
- Secondary: Liam Walker
- Use when the article is about a walkable route, neighborhood experience, or how to build a half-day itinerary.
